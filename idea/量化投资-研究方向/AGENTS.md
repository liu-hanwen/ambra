# Research Direction: Quantitative Investing

## Background

Quantitative investing applies mathematical models, statistics, and software systems to financial markets. This direction focuses on extracting research-backed strategies from academic papers and market data, with particular interest in A-share equities, crypto markets, and cross-market arbitrage.

## Focus Areas

- **Strategy families:** momentum, mean reversion, factor investing, high-frequency trading, and pair trading
- **Technical toolkit:** time-series analysis, machine learning for finance, and reinforcement learning for trading systems
- **Risk management:** maximum drawdown control, volatility modeling, and tail-risk handling
- **Data sources:** market microstructure data plus alternative data such as satellite imagery or news sentiment
- **Execution detail:** slippage modeling, transaction cost modeling, and capacity analysis

## Desired Idea Types

- transfer an academic finding into an underexplored market or asset class
- combine methods from different domains, such as NLP plus quantitative trading
- fix a known weakness in an existing strategy family
- design reproducible backtesting experiments
- propose new data acquisition or feature-engineering approaches

## Out of Scope

- traditional fundamental investing based on metrics such as PE or ROE
- long-hold strategies with holding periods longer than one year
- macroeconomic forecasting
